Dr.APJ Abdul Kalam

The Missile man of India

Here's a time line of Dr.Apj Abdul Kalam

  • 1931 - on 15th of october he born at madras India
  • 1954 - Graduates in physics from Saint Joseph's college,Tiruchirappallli.
  • 1960 - Joins DRDO as scientist , He completes his degree in Aeronautical Engineering from Madras institute of Technology(MIT) and joins the Defence Research and Development organization as scientist.
  • 1969 - He moves to ISRO(Indian Space and Research Organization) where he helms India's first Satellite Launch Vehicle.
  • 1980 - Lead's India's entry in the Space club , He leads india's efforts to enter the space club by putting the Rohini satellite in Earth's orbit.
  • 1980-1990 - Work as chief of the Integrated Guided Missile Development Programme , under his lead India develop two deadly missile Named Prithni and Agni.
  • 1981 - Recieves the Padma Bhushan for his contribution in science and technology.
  • 1990 - Honoured with Padma Vibhushan , which is second highest civilian honour award.
  • 1992-1999 - Appointed India's chief Scientific Adviser to the prime minister.
  • 1997 - Honoured with the Bharat Ratna award , which is India's first civilian award for his contribution in scientific redearch and modernisation of defence technology in India.
  • 1998 - Spearheads the Pokhran mission , which is India's first neculear testing mission.
  • 2002-2007 - Elected as India's 11th president
  • 2012 - Lauch 'what can i give movement' which is against the corruption.
  • 2015 - At the age of 88 on july 27th , He Passes away in Shilong , Meghalaya due to cardiac arrest while delivering a lecture at IIM Shilong.
